About Play Therapy

What is Play Therapy?

Play therapy is a form of therapy used primarily for children (3-12 years old). That’s because children may not be able to process their own emotions or articulate problems to parents or other adults. Play therapist can provide a safe environment where children can explore their feelings, thoughts, experiences and behaviors through the use of creative arts and play, without the fear of failure or judgment. The therapy is non-judgmental and accepting, allowing the child to safely explore traumas or emotions that they may otherwise not be able to be put into words.

Proven Method

PTUK's (Play Therapy United Kingdom) clinical evidence base, containing over 12,000 cases, shows that between 77% and 84% of children show a positive change through the use of creative arts and play therapy when delivered to PTUK standards. The more severe the problem, the higher the percentage of children that show a positive change.

How long does it take for the parent to see improvement from a child who receives creative arts and play therapy?

Parents should refrain from anticipating immediate resolutions to their children's issues, as these problems are typically longstanding and stem from various factors. Gradual improvement in the child's condition can be observed with consistent therapy sessions. Typically, parents report noticing some improvement from their children after 4-5 sessions, while clinically significant changes may become evident after approximately 12 sessions. Active implementation of the therapist's recommendations in daily life and parenting can expedite the pace of progress.

Do I need to commit to a minimum number of therapy sessions for my child?

It's not compulsory for parents to commit to a minimum number of sessions, although the therapist typically suggests a recommended number of sessions based on the initial assessment. Parents may sometimes be concerned about whether their children will want to continue therapy, or find it difficult to bring them for regular sessions. Therefore, parents have the option to pay for individual therapy sessions for their children.

What will happen after submitting the contact form on this site?

The therapist will contact you through email to understand the issues of your child and answer your queries. If parent has decided to let his/her child receive play therapy from Rainbow Kids Counselling & Play Therapy, a form called Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaires (SDQ) will be sent to parent. SDQ is to help the therapist assess the strengths and difficulties of the child. The therapist will arrange a time for a session with parent to understand more about the background information for intake assessment which will be conducted on Zoom. When sufficient information is obtained, the therapist will recommend the number of sessions based on the risk of the child.

Normally 6 – 12 weekly sessions will be recommended first. Therapist will continue to assess the needs of the child during therapy and discuss with parent for further intervention if necessary.

Parent can also consult therapist how to support the child at home in a separate parent consultation session.

Is Rainbow Kids a home-based therapy service? What are the benefits of home-based therapy?

Yes, Rainbow Kids is a legally registered company in Singapore and meets the requirements of URA as home-based business. This setup allows the therapist to reduce rent expense and offer services at more reasonable fees. Additionally, therapist can schedule appointments to ensure ample time for sanitizing toys after each session. Clients do not need to wait once their appointment is confirmed. Many children also feel more comfortable and less stressed when receiving therapy in a home setting.
